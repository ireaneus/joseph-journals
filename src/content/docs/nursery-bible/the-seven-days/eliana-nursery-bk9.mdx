*For Eliana — "My God has answered"*  
*made in the image of the One above,*  
*blessed by the God of power and love,*  
*the crown of all He's thinking of.*  

*Genesis 1:26–31 · ESV*

---

## Page 1 — Let Us Make
👐

Then God paused —  
and said *Let us,*  
as if the making  
mattered most,  
as if He leaned in  
close and near  
to make the one  
He held most dear.

> *"Then God said, 'Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.'"*  
> **Genesis 1:26a (ESV)**

> *Every other day God simply spoke — and things appeared. But here He paused and said let us. The making of mankind was different, personal, deliberate. You were not spoken from a distance. You were made up close.*

---

## Page 2 — In Our Image
🪞

Not like the fish,  
not like the birds —  
something deeper  
than all the words:  
a living soul  
that thinks and sees,  
that loves and wonders,  
bends its knees.

> *"So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them."*  
> **Genesis 1:27 (ESV)**

> *To bear God's image is to carry something of Him into the world — His wisdom, His care, His capacity to love. When God looks at you, He sees His own reflection looking back.*

---

## Page 3 — He Blessed Them
🙌

He didn't just make them  
and step away —  
He opened His mouth  
and began to say  
words of blessing,  
words of grace,  
words that filled  
that holy place.

> *"And God blessed them."*  
> **Genesis 1:28a (ESV)**

> *Before He gave them anything to do, He blessed them. The first thing God speaks over mankind is not a command — it is a blessing. He leads with love. He always has.*

---

## Page 4 — Fruitful and Filled
🌍

*Be fruitful,* He said,  
*and multiply,*  
*fill the earth  
beneath My sky —*  
*tend what I've made,  
hold it with care,*  
*I am giving you  
all that is there.*

> *"And God said to them, 'Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth and subdue it, and have dominion over the fish of the sea and over the birds of the heavens and over every living thing that moves on the earth.'"*  
> **Genesis 1:28b (ESV)**

> *God gave mankind the earth as a gift and a trust — to fill it, to tend it, to care for what He made. Dominion is not carelessness; it is stewardship. To rule as God rules is to serve with love.*

---

## Page 5 — Every Green Thing
🌿

He gave them food  
from plant and tree,  
He gave the creatures  
all they'd need —  
a world so full,  
so rich, so wide,  
with every good thing  
spread inside.

> *"And God said, 'Behold, I have given you every plant yielding seed that is on the face of all the earth, and every tree with seed in its fruit. You shall have them for food. And to every beast of the earth and to every bird of the heavens and to everything that creeps on the earth, everything that has the breath of life, I have given every green plant for food.' And it was so."*  
> **Genesis 1:29–30 (ESV)**

> *God thought of everything — every creature, every need, every morning meal. He is a provider before He is ever asked. His generosity was written into the world before the first hunger.*

---

## Page 6 — Very Good
✨

He looked at all  
that He had made —  
the light, the sky,  
the land, the glade,  
the creatures wild,  
the man, the wife —  
*very good* —  
this gift of life.

> *"And God saw everything that he had made, and behold, it was very good. And there was evening and there was morning, the sixth day."*  
> **Genesis 1:31 (ESV)**

> *Not just good — very good. The only day God says it this way. The whole creation, with mankind at its crown, was exactly what He meant. And you, little one, are part of what He calls very good.*

---

## ✦ Closing

> *"For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works,*  
> *which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them."*  
> **Ephesians 2:10 · ESV**
