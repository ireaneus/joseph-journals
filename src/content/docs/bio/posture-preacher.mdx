## Ezekiel — Prostrate Before God

The priest was thirty years old, an exile five years into captivity, standing on the bank of the river Chebar when the storm wind came out of the north. Flashing fire, burning wheels, living beings, and above them all a throne — and above the throne, a radiance like a rainbow in the clouds on a rainy day.

> **Ezekiel 1:28–2:2**  
> *"As the appearance of the rainbow in the clouds on a rainy day, so was the appearance of the surrounding radiance. Such was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD. And when I saw it, I fell on my face and heard a voice speaking."*  
> *Then He said to me, 'Son of man, stand on your feet that I may speak with you!' As He spoke to me the Spirit entered me and set me on my feet; and I heard Him speaking to me.*

When Ezekiel saw the likeness of the glory of the LORD, he did not reach for his notes. He fell on his face. And he stayed there until the Spirit of God set him on his feet — because a man flattened by glory cannot stand up on his own, and God does not send men who stand up on their own. The Spirit raises, empowers, and sends. The servant presents himself:

> **Romans 12:1**  
> *"Therefore I urge you, brethren, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies a living and holy sacrifice, acceptable to God, which is your spiritual service of worship."*

The posture of a preacher begins there — on his face. Some called men argued: Moses answered the burning bush with objections, and Jonah ran for the sea. The men traced here show the better posture: Isaiah, Jeremiah, John, Paul — and one preacher of righteousness who never spoke at all.

## Isaiah — Cleansed Lips Before Speaking

> **Isaiah 6:5–7**  
> *"Then I said, 'Woe is me, for I am ruined! Because I am a man of unclean lips,*  
> *And I live among a people of unclean lips;*  
> *For my eyes have seen the King, the LORD of hosts.'*  
> *Then one of the seraphim flew to me with a burning coal in his hand which he had taken from the altar with tongs.*  
> *He touched my mouth with it and said, 'Behold, this has touched your lips; and your iniquity is taken away and your sin is forgiven.'"*

When Isaiah saw the holiness of the Lord, his first words were confession: *"I am a man of unclean lips."* Only after the coal touched his mouth did he answer the call — *"Here am I. Send me!"* (**Isaiah 6:8**).

## Jeremiah — Words Placed, Not Produced

> **Jeremiah 1:6–9**  
> *Then I said, "Alas, Lord GOD! Behold, I do not know how to speak,*  
> *Because I am a youth."*  
> *But the LORD said to me, "Do not say, 'I am a youth,'*  
> *Because everywhere I send you, you shall go,*  
> *And all that I command you, you shall speak.*  
> *Do not be afraid of them,*  
> *For I am with you to deliver you," declares the LORD.*  
> *Then the LORD stretched out His hand and touched my mouth,*  
> *and the LORD said to me, "Behold, I have put My words in your mouth."*

Jeremiah confessed weakness. And the Lord answered the weakness with His own hand — He touched the young man's mouth and put His own words there. The servant's task is to speak what God has given. The pulpit is a delivery point, not a workshop.

## The Charge — Do Not Omit a Word

> **Jeremiah 26:2**  
> *"Thus says the LORD, 'Stand in the court of the LORD'S house, and speak to all the cities of Judah who have come to worship in the LORD'S house all the words that I have commanded you to speak to them. Do not omit a word!'"*

Pastor, this is the charge, and it has one measure: every word. The words about wrath alongside the words about grace. The texts that empty pews alongside the texts that fill them. The prophet's responsibility was to deliver all the words God commanded — including the words the audience came hoping to avoid. If the people under your preaching have never once been made uncomfortable by the whole counsel of God, the omissions are yours to answer for.

## John — At His Feet Like a Dead Man

> **Revelation 1:9–11, 17–19**  
> *"I, John, your brother and fellow partaker in the tribulation and kingdom and perseverance which are in Jesus, was on the island called Patmos because of the word of God and the testimony of Jesus. I was in the Spirit on the Lord's Day, and I heard behind me a loud voice like the sound of a trumpet, saying, 'Write in a book what you see, and send it to the seven churches…'*  
> *When I saw Him, I fell at His feet like a dead man. And He placed His right hand on me, saying, 'Do not be afraid; I am the first and the last, and the living One; and I was dead, and behold, I am alive forevermore, and I have the keys of death and of Hades. Therefore write the things which you have seen, and the things which are, and the things which will take place after these things.'"*

John had walked with Jesus for three years. He had leaned against Him at supper. Sixty years later, one sight of the risen Christ in glory put him on the ground like a dead man. The most seasoned pastor alive that day — and all his years did nothing to keep him on his feet. Then the right hand came down on him: *Do not be afraid. Write.* The command to proclaim came to a man face-down. It still does.

## Paul — The Whole Counsel of God

> **Acts 20:27**  
> *"For I did not shrink from declaring to you the whole purpose of God."*

Paul stood before the Ephesian elders knowing he would never see their faces again, and this is what he claimed as his faithfulness: he had declared the whole purpose of God. He preached all of it and left the reception in God's hands. That is the standard he handed to every elder in the room — and to every elder reading this.

## Noah — The Preacher Who Never Spoke

Search the account of Noah for a sermon and you will come up empty. Scripture records no words from him at all — yet Peter calls him a preacher of righteousness.

> **2 Peter 2:5**  
> *"and did not spare the ancient world, but preserved Noah, a preacher of righteousness, with seven others, when He brought a flood upon the world of the ungodly."*

The sermon was a hundred years of hammer blows. He built while they laughed, and the ark itself declared both the judgment coming and the door still open. He endured the ridicule the way his greater descendant would one day endure it — silent.

> **Isaiah 53:7**  
> *"He was oppressed and He was afflicted, yet He did not open His mouth; like a lamb that is led to slaughter, and like a sheep that is silent before its shearers, so He did not open His mouth."*

The clay does not talk back to the potter (**Romans 9:20**). It obeys the One who formed it. Some of the loudest preaching in Scripture never made a sound.

## The Posture of a Preacher

![Broken Cisterns that hold no water](/joseph-journals/images/brokencisterns.webp)

The preacher offers himself as a vessel. The Spirit supplies what the vessel lacks. And faithfulness means the whole message, delivered without omission — in words, and in a life that keeps preaching after the words stop. Noah never preached a recorded sermon. His obedience was the sermon.
