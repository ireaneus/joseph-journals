I am a husband, father, grandfather, and a student of Scripture who has come to love the Lord Jesus Christ more deeply over time. I have lived most of my life in ordinary places — family responsibilities, daily work, quiet study, and the slow lessons God teaches outside the spotlight.

## Broken Cisterns

> **Jeremiah 2:13**  
> *"For My people have committed two evils: They have forsaken Me, The fountain of living waters, To hew for themselves cisterns, Broken cisterns That can hold no water."*

It is easy to read that verse and think of people who turned to idols, false religions, or worldly pursuits. I read it and see myself. I have sought fulfillment in work, in the approval of others, even in my own efforts to serve God. Every one of them is a broken cistern — it holds no water. The living water is Christ, and He alone satisfies.

## Learning to Listen

There was a time when I was more eager to speak than to listen. Zeal ran ahead of wisdom, as it often does in those who begin to grow in knowledge. The Lord has been kind to reshape that posture.

I am still learning that ministry begins with hearing people well — asking thoughtful questions, understanding burdens, and speaking truth with humility, gentleness, and love.

Sometimes the most useful servant in the room is the one who listens carefully.

## A Lesson in Evangelism

During my studies in the Contenders Discipleship Initiative, one session focused on sharing the gospel clearly. Around that time I sat down with others for a card game I usually lost, a simple gospel outline fresh in my mind. Before the first hand I prayed privately: if the Lord wanted it shared, He would need to open the way — enough winning hands to present each part of the outline.

Before we started, I asked the table two questions: *Who is Jesus?* and *What is your relationship with God?* I let them answer the first, and quietly reflect on the second.

As the game unfolded, I kept winning hands. With each hand won, I shared another section of the presentation. By the end of the game, I had gone through the full message and even won overall — something uncommon enough that everyone noticed.

I left with mixed feelings. Some responses were positive, yet the delivery felt rough and overly scripted. Looking back, the Lord was teaching me something deeper: faithfulness is my responsibility, while the results belong to Him. It became a practice session in learning to share the gospel more naturally, and to truly listen to people's stories before rushing in with a prepared message.

> **Proverbs 16:33**  
> *"The lot is cast into the lap, but its every decision is from the Lord."*

Even now, I am still learning not only *what* to say, but *how* to say it.

## Training and Study

By God's grace, I recently completed the Contenders Discipleship Initiative program, a season of structured study covering Scripture, theology, church history, evangelism, teaching, and ministry practice.

The training strengthened foundations, deepened convictions, and left me grateful for the faithful teachers who labor to equip the saints. It also confirmed how much there is still to learn, and how much humility the learning requires.
