> **Psalm 25:9**  
> *"He leads the humble in justice, and He teaches the humble His way."*

*I am a husband, father, grandfather, and a student of Scripture who has come to love the Lord Jesus Christ more deeply over time. Much of life has been lived in ordinary places—family responsibilities, daily work, quiet study, and the slow lessons God often teaches outside the spotlight.*

This page is not meant to magnify a person, but to acknowledge the patience and mercy of God through years of growth, correction, and grace.

## Learning to Listen

There was a time when I was more eager to speak than to listen. Like many who begin to grow in knowledge, zeal can run ahead of wisdom. The Lord has been kind to reshape that posture.

I am still learning that ministry is not merely having something to say. It is learning to hear people well, to ask thoughtful questions, to understand burdens, and to speak truth with humility, gentleness, and love.

Sometimes the most useful servant in the room is not the loudest one, but the one who listens carefully.

## A Lesson in Evangelism

During my studies in the Contenders Discipleship Initiative, one session focused on evangelism and sharing the gospel clearly. Around that time, **I was with others playing a card game I was not known for winning.** I had prepared a simple gospel presentation outline, and I felt the Lord prompting me to share it if the opportunity arose.

Before the game began, I prayed privately that if the Lord desired me to share what I had learned, He would need to open the way. I asked that I might win enough hands to present each part of the gospel outline I had prepared.

Before we started, I asked the table two questions: *Who is Jesus?* and *What is your relationship with God?* I let them answer the first, and quietly reflect on the second.

As the game unfolded, I unexpectedly kept winning hands. With each hand won, I shared another section of the presentation. By the end of the game, I had gone through the full message and even won overall—something uncommon enough that everyone noticed.

I left with mixed feelings. Some responses were positive, yet the delivery felt rough and overly scripted. Looking back, the Lord was teaching me something deeper: faithfulness is my responsibility, while the results belong to Him. It became a practice session in learning to share the gospel more naturally, and to truly listen to people’s stories before rushing in with a prepared message.

> **Proverbs 16:33**  
> *"The lot is cast into the lap, but its every decision is from the Lord."*

Even now, I am still learning not only *what* to say, but *how* to say it.

## Training and Study

By God's grace, I recently completed the Contenders Discipleship Initiative program, a season of structured study covering Scripture, theology, church history, evangelism, teaching, and ministry practice.

That training did not make me an expert, but it strengthened foundations, deepened convictions, and increased gratitude for faithful teachers who labor to equip the saints. It also confirmed that there is always more to learn and greater need for humility.

## Why This Site Exists

This website is a place to gather reflections, studies, sermons, notes, and testimonies of God's providence. It is part journal, part workshop, and part reminder that the Lord continues to shape His people in every season of life.
