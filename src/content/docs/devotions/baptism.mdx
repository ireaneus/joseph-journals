## To Michael and Samuel, on the day of your baptism

Boys, before you enter the water, I want to ask you a question: How does God speak to you?

*Take a moment with that before you read on. Whoever you are, wherever you are standing — answer it first.*

---

## He Speaks in Many Ways

Sometimes it is a pit in your stomach warning you that what you are about to do is wrong. Sometimes it is a reflection on something you lived through, seen differently from the far side of it. Sometimes He uses friends — even strangers passing by, angels disguised as people. But most of all, His desire is to speak to you directly from His Word. And when you hear Him there, you will know it: a stirring in your soul, confirming the Scripture in front of you, telling you He is talking to you.

That stirring is what brought you to this water.

Scripture shows how He likes to speak. When Elijah stood on the mountain, the wind tore the rocks apart, then the earthquake came, then the fire — and the Lord was in none of them. He was in the gentle blowing that came after **(1 Kings 19:11–12)**.

And He spoke to a boy who carried the same name one of you carries. Samuel was young when the Lord called his name in the dark, and he learned the answer every servant of God has to learn:

> **1 Samuel 3:10 (ESV)**  
> *"And the LORD came and stood, calling as at other times, 'Samuel! Samuel!' And Samuel said, 'Speak, for your servant hears.'"*

That is the posture I want for both of you, for the rest of your lives: ears open, Bible open, ready to hear and obey. You will not always answer Him with words. Most of the time the answer is your life — the thing you walk away from, the person you stop for, the obedience nobody sees. Samuel answered in the dark with one sentence. He spent the rest of his life answering with everything else.

## You Followed Jesus Into the Water

John stood in the Jordan preaching repentance — repent, and be baptized. Jesus was perfect. He had no sin to repent of. But He stepped into the water anyway, because He was demonstrating what everyone who follows Him must do. His baptism was the first act of His public ministry. Watch what happened when He came up:

> **Matthew 3:16–17 (ESV)**  
> *"And when Jesus was baptized, immediately he went up from the water, and behold, the heavens were opened to him, and he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and coming to rest on him; and behold, a voice from heaven said, 'This is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased.'"*

The Spirit came to rest on Him, and from that day the Spirit was with Him. The Father spoke. The Son obeyed. Your baptism follows His — the water saves no one; Christ already did that. Baptism is the first act of obedience for everyone who follows Him. You stood in front of everyone and said yes out loud.

## The Road Will Be Hard

I will not send you off with only sunshine. Jesus was honest with His disciples the night before He died, and I want to be honest with you:

> **John 16:33 (ESV)**  
> *"I have said these things to you, that in me you may have peace. In the world you will have tribulation. But take heart; I have overcome the world."*

Paul said it even plainer: *"through many tribulations we must enter the kingdom of God"* **(Acts 14:22 ESV)**. The narrow path is a hard path. But the One who overcame the world walks it with you, every step.

## The Words You Are Walking Toward

Keep walking. Keep listening for His voice the way Samuel listened. Stay faithful in the small things, because the small things are where faithfulness lives. And at the end of the road, there is a sentence waiting for every servant who stayed the course:

> **Matthew 25:21 (ESV)**  
> *"His master said to him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant. You have been faithful over a little; I will set you over much. Enter into the joy of your master.'"*

That is the goal, boys. You heard His voice, and you obeyed — the water says so. Now keep listening, all the way home.
