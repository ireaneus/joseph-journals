## *Before I wake, I pray —*

Before the light comes, before the house stirs, my soul stirs for You.

*I rise before dawn and cry for help; I wait for Your words. My eyes anticipate the night watches, that I may meditate on Your word.* **(Psalm 119:147–148)**

This is where the day begins. Here, in the dark, before a single word of my own forms — I wait for His.

*The Lord God has given Me the tongue of disciples, that I may know how to sustain the weary one with a word. He awakens Me morning by morning, He awakens My ear to listen as a disciple.* **(Isaiah 50:4)**

So He leads me to a place the soul recognizes before the mind does.

*He makes me lie down in green pastures; He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul.* **(Psalm 23:2–3)**

Beside still water. The soul settling, the breath slowing, the broken cistern arriving at the only fountain that never runs dry.

And here, beside the water, I have only one thing to say.

*My soul waits in silence for God only; from Him is my salvation.* **(Psalm 62:1)**

The way Elijah waited on the mountain, having survived the wind that broke the rocks, and the earthquake, and the fire. And then — a sound of a gentle blowing. **(1 Kings 19:11–12)**

*The Lord is good to those who wait for Him, to the person who seeks Him. It is good that he waits silently for the salvation of the Lord.* **(Lamentations 3:25–26)**

This is how the morning begins. Waiting. Listening.

## Come to the Waters

I come to You as a broken cistern — cracked, empty, unable to hold on my own what only You can give.

*For My people have committed two evils: They have forsaken Me, the fountain of living waters, to hew for themselves cisterns, broken cisterns that can hold no water.* **(Jeremiah 2:13)**

And the Father calls out to those that thirst.

*Ho! Every one who thirsts, come to the waters; and you who have no money come, buy and eat. Come, buy wine and milk without money and without cost.* **(Isaiah 55:1)**

But with lips parched I try to speak — I am broken, I cannot hold any water, I cannot speak.

*O God, You are my God; I shall seek You earnestly; My soul thirsts for You, my flesh yearns for You, in a dry and weary land where there is no water.* **(Psalm 63:1)**

And so I come. The way the woman came to the well — knowing she was thirsty. And like her, I cry out —

*Sir, give me this water, so I will not be thirsty.* **(John 4:15)**

And He answers —

*Everyone who drinks of this water will thirst again; but whoever drinks of the water that I will give him shall never thirst; but the water that I will give him will become in him a well of water springing up to eternal life.* **(John 4:13–14)**

And He stands and cries out —

*If anyone is thirsty, let him come to Me and drink.* **(John 7:37)**

## A Prayer of Absolute Surrender

The soul has been filled. Now it is offered back.

He speaks — and His voice is personal.

*"Because he has loved Me, therefore I will deliver him; I will set him securely on high, because he has known My name. He will call upon Me, and I will answer him; I will be with him in trouble; I will rescue him and honor him."* **(Psalm 91:14–15)**

And so I respond —

*O Lord, You have searched me and known me. You know when I sit down and when I rise up; You understand my thought from afar.* **(Psalm 139:1–2)**

*By Your grace, I desire to do Your will in everything, every moment of this day. Not a word upon my tongue but for Your glory. Not a movement of my spirit but according to Your blessed will. I give myself absolutely to You — to let You work in me both to will and to do of Your good pleasure, as You have promised.*

He speaks again —

*"I have loved you with an everlasting love; Therefore I have drawn you with lovingkindness."* **(Jeremiah 31:3)**

And I release the day into His hands —

*"But as for me, I trust in You, O Lord, I say, 'You are my God.' My times are in Your hand."* **(Psalm 31:14–15)**

## The Instruction of the Lord

The soul has waited. It has been filled. It has surrendered. Now the Spirit moves.

*Then the Spirit entered me and set me on my feet; and I heard Him speaking to me.* **(Ezekiel 2:2)**

He raises what was prostrate — and then He speaks.

*Then the Lord stretched out His hand and touched my mouth, and the Lord said to me, "Behold, I have put My words in your mouth."* **(Jeremiah 1:9)**

The words are His. Placed there. The servant brings only the mouth that was offered. And the charge is clear —

*"Stand in the court of the Lord's house, and speak to all the cities of Judah who have come to worship in the Lord's house all the words that I have commanded you to speak to them. Do not omit a word."* **(Jeremiah 26:2)**

And so we answer —

*"Then I heard the voice of the Lord, saying, 'Whom shall I send, and who will go for Us?' Then I said, 'Here am I. Send me!'"* **(Isaiah 6:8)**

*"But if I say I will not remember Him or speak anymore in His name, then in my heart it becomes like a burning fire shut up in my bones; and I am weary of holding it in, and I cannot endure it."* **(Jeremiah 20:9)**

## A Prayer for Spouse and Family

*Adapted from David Gibbs' Daily Prayer List for His Wife*

The commissioned servant now turns toward his household. The way Job rose early — before the day touched his children — and stood before God on their behalf.

*When the days of feasting had completed their cycle, Job would send and consecrate them, rising up early in the morning and offering burnt offerings according to the number of them all; for Job said, "Perhaps my sons have sinned and cursed God in their hearts." Thus Job did continually.* **(Job 1:5)**

And so I pray —

*"…that you may be filled with the knowledge of His will in all spiritual wisdom and understanding."* **(Colossians 1:9)**

That you may walk in His steps —

*"The mind of man plans his way, but the Lord directs his steps."* **(Proverbs 16:9)**

And that the peace of God will guard your heart —

*"Be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God. And the peace of God, which surpasses all comprehension, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."* **(Philippians 4:6–7)**

That you would be still and hear His voice —

*"…and after the fire a sound of a gentle blowing."* **(1 Kings 19:12)**

Reveal to them Your path of life —

*"You will make known to me the path of life; In Your presence is fullness of joy; In Your right hand there are pleasures forever."* **(Psalm 16:11)**

Restore to them each morning the gifts You have given them, that they may use them for Your glory —

*"As each one has received a special gift, employ it in serving one another as good stewards of the manifold grace of God. Whoever speaks, is to do so as one who is speaking the utterances of God; whoever serves is to do so as one who is serving by the strength which God supplies; so that in all things God may be glorified through Jesus Christ."* **(1 Peter 4:10–11)**

And bring them to rest in You —

*"Come to Me, all who are weary and heavy-laden, and I will give you rest. Take My yoke upon you and learn from Me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls."* **(Matthew 11:28–29)**

## Before the First Step

*"Teach me to do Your will, for You are my God; Let Your good Spirit lead me on level ground."* **(Psalm 143:10)**

You are clay. He is the Potter. The day is His to govern and yours to walk in faithful obedience, one moment at a time.

Go. And walk in the path of life.
