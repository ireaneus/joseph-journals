## *Before I wake, I pray —*

Before the light comes, before the house stirs, my soul stirs for You.

*I rise before dawn and cry for help; I wait for Your words. My eyes anticipate the night watches, that I may meditate on Your word.* **(Psalm 119:147–148)**

This is where the day begins. Here, in the dark, before a single word of my own forms — I wait for His.

And He answers. He answers the way He always has with those He calls His own — as a teacher to a student, as a shepherd to a lamb still finding its feet.

*The Lord God has given Me the tongue of disciples, that I may know how to sustain the weary one with a word. He awakens Me morning by morning, He awakens My ear to listen as a disciple.* **(Isaiah 50:4)**

So He leads me. The way He has always led the ones He loves — away to a place the soul recognizes before the mind does.

*He makes me lie down in green pastures; He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul.* **(Psalm 23:2–3)**

Beside still water. The soul settling, the breath slowing, the broken cistern arriving at the only fountain that never runs dry.

And here, beside the water, I have only one thing to say.

*My soul waits in silence for God only; from Him is my salvation.* **(Psalm 62:1)**

Only waiting — the way Elijah waited on the mountain, having survived the wind that broke the rocks, and the earthquake, and the fire. And then — a sound of a gentle blowing. **(1 Kings 19:11–12)**

That is where He is. In the still small voice. In the quiet after everything loud has passed.

*The Lord is good to those who wait for Him, to the person who seeks Him. It is good that he waits silently for the salvation of the Lord.* **(Lamentations 3:25–26)**

This is how the morning begins. Waiting. Listening. Held.

## Come to the Waters

My soul knows what it is. I come to You as a broken cistern — cracked, empty, unable to hold on my own what only You can give.

*For My people have committed two evils: They have forsaken Me, the fountain of living waters, to hew for themselves cisterns, broken cisterns that can hold no water.* **(Jeremiah 2:13)**

And the Father calls out to those that thirst.

*Ho! Every one who thirsts, come to the waters; and you who have no money come, buy and eat. Come, buy wine and milk without money and without cost.* **(Isaiah 55:1)**

But with lips parched I try to speak — I am broken, I cannot hold any water, I cannot speak.

*O God, You are my God; I shall seek You earnestly; My soul thirsts for You, my flesh yearns for You, in a dry and weary land where there is no water.* **(Psalm 63:1)**

And so I come. The way the woman came to the well — knowing she was thirsty. And like her, I cry out —

*Sir, give me this water, so I will not be thirsty.* **(John 4:15)**

And He answers —

*Everyone who drinks of this water will thirst again; but whoever drinks of the water that I will give him shall never thirst; but the water that I will give him will become in him a well of water springing up to eternal life.* **(John 4:13–14)**

And He stands and cries out —

*If anyone is thirsty, let him come to Me and drink.* **(John 7:37)**

## A Prayer of Absolute Surrender

**Adapted from Andrew Murray**

*Lord, I pray before I am fully awake — put Your Word upon my lips.*

*By Your grace, I desire to do Your will in everything, every moment of this day.*

*Not a word upon my tongue but for Your glory. Not a movement of my spirit but according to Your blessed will.*

*I give myself absolutely to You — to let You work in me both to will and to do of Your good pleasure, as You have promised.*

> **Psalm 119:148**  
> *"My eyes are awake before the watches of the night, that I may meditate on your promises."*  
>
> **Philippians 2:13**  
> *"…for it is God who is at work in you, both to will and to work for His good pleasure."*
>
> **Romans 12:1**  
> *"Therefore I urge you, brethren, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies a living and holy sacrifice, acceptable to God, which is your spiritual service of worship."*
>
> **1 Kings 20:4**  
> *"It is according to your word, my lord, O king; I am yours, and all that I have."*

*Lord, stir in me a great hunger and thirst for Your righteousness, for Your Word, and for Your presence. Let nothing else satisfy but You alone.*

> **Psalm 63:1**  
> *"O God, You are my God; I shall seek You earnestly; My soul thirsts for You, my flesh yearns for You, In a dry and weary land where there is no water."*

## Morning Scripture Readings

These passages are the stakes driven into the ground before the hours begin.

**The Path of Life**

> **Psalm 16:11**  
> *"You will make known to me the path of life; In Your presence is fullness of joy; In Your right hand there are pleasures forever."*

**Rest for the Weary**

> **Matthew 11:28–30**  
> *"Come to Me, all who are weary and heavy-laden, and I will give you rest. Take My yoke upon you and learn from Me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For My yoke is easy and My burden is light."*

**Blessed in the Law of the Lord**

> **Psalm 119:1–2**  
> *"How blessed are those whose way is blameless, who walk in the law of the Lord. How blessed are those who observe His testimonies, who seek Him with all their heart."*  

**The Stewardship of Each Day**

> **1 Peter 4:10–11**  
> *"As each one has received a special gift, employ it in serving one another as good stewards of the manifold grace of God. Whoever speaks, is to do so as one who is speaking the utterances of God; whoever serves is to do so as one who is serving by the strength which God supplies; so that in all things God may be glorified through Jesus Christ, to whom belongs the glory and dominion forever and ever. Amen."*

**Against Anxiety**

> **Philippians 4:6–7**  
> *"Be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God. And the peace of God, which surpasses all comprehension, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."*

**Teachable Before God**

> **Psalm 143:10**  
> *"Teach me to do Your will, For You are my God; Let Your good Spirit lead me on level ground."*

**God Does What He Pleases**

> **Psalm 135:6**  
> *"Whatever the Lord pleases, He does, In heaven and in earth, in the seas and in all deeps."*

This last verse is not merely a comfort — it is a foundation. The God to whom you surrender your day is the God who sovereignly governs every moment of it. Not a sparrow falls outside His decree. You are not yielding to chance. You are yielding to the One who already holds all things.

## A Prayer for Spouse and Family

**Adapted from David Gibbs' Daily Prayer List for His Wife**

This is the most important work of the morning — the vertical before the horizontal, the intercession that undergirds every relationship in the home.

**Pray today that God would grant your spouse and family:**

**1. To be filled with the knowledge of His will**

> **Colossians 1:9**  
> *"…that you may be filled with the knowledge of His will in all spiritual wisdom and understanding."*

**2. Ordered steps and a directed path**

> **Proverbs 16:9**  
> *"The mind of man plans his way, But the Lord directs his steps."*

**3. Great peace that passes all understanding**

> **Philippians 4:6–7**  
> *"Be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God. And the peace of God, which surpasses all comprehension, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."*

**4. A heart sensitive and receptive to His voice**

> **1 Kings 19:12**  
> *"…and after the fire a sound of a gentle blowing."*

**5. That they would diligently seek Him**

> **Psalm 63:1**  
> *"O God, You are my God; I shall seek You earnestly; My soul thirsts for You, my flesh yearns for You, In a dry and weary land where there is no water."*  

**6. A stirred hunger and thirst for His righteousness**

> **Isaiah 55:1–2**  
> *"Ho! Every one who thirsts, come to the waters…"*

**7. Magnificent joy**

> **Psalm 16:11**  
> *"You will make known to me the path of life; In Your presence is fullness of joy; In Your right hand there are pleasures forever."*

**8. God's contentment**

> **Psalm 131:2**

**9. That they would faithfully use and nurture every gift entrusted to them**

> **1 Peter 4:10–11**  
> *"As each one has received a special gift, employ it in serving one another as good stewards of the manifold grace of God…"*

**10. That they would walk more closely with Him**

**11. Time alone with God in devotion and prayer**

> **Psalm 46:10**

**12. That they would surrender all that they are and all that they have**

> **1 Kings 20:4**  
> *"It is according to your word, my lord, O king; I am yours, and all that I have."*

**13. Perfect rest, knowing they are held in the hands of the Creator**

> **Matthew 11:28–29**

## Before the First Step

The day will come with its demands. It always does. But before the first step, before the first word — this:

> **Psalm 143:10**  
> *"Teach me to do Your will, For You are my God; Let Your good Spirit lead me on level ground."*

You are clay. He is the Potter. The day is not yours to manage — it is His to govern and yours to walk in faithful obedience, one moment at a time.

Go. And walk in the path of life.
