## Coming Out of Chapter 37

Ezekiel 37 closed with the Lord making an everlasting covenant of peace with His restored and reunited people Israel. That covenant was not incidental. It was the Lord's solemn, unconditional pledge — that nothing would take Israel's peace away in the Kingdom, that His sanctuary would be in their midst forever, and that the nations would come to know Him through what He does for His people.

> **Ezekiel 37:26-28 (NASB1995)**
> *"I will make a covenant of peace with them; it will be an everlasting covenant with them. And I will place them and multiply them, and will set My sanctuary in their midst forever. My dwelling place also will be with them; and I will be their God, and they will be My people. And the nations will know that I am the LORD who sanctifies Israel, when My sanctuary is in their midst forever."*

Now in Chapter 38, the Lord does something that at first appears to contradict everything He just promised. He summons an enemy. He puts hooks in the jaws of a vast northern coalition and draws them down against the very Israel He swore to protect.

The apparent contradiction resolves the moment we ask the question the text itself raises: *What is the purpose of the war?*

A covenant of peace only proves itself when peace is threatened. The Lord promised to protect Israel from her enemies — but that promise cannot be demonstrated in a vacuum. There must be an enemy to defeat. And so the Lord, in His sovereignty, ordains the very invasion that will give Him opportunity to show Himself faithful to the covenant He made in Chapter 37. What the Lord begins in Chapter 38 He completes in Chapter 39, and the result is not merely a military victory. It is the sanctification of His name before every nation on earth.

This is the context. Without it, Ezekiel 38 reads as an isolated and puzzling war prophecy. Within it, the chapter is the Lord's ordered demonstration of His own faithfulness — a proof on the world stage that the covenant-keeping God means exactly what He says.

## The Four Views — Setting the Interpretive Question

Before entering the text, the question of *when* this war occurs must be addressed, because the timing governs how every detail is read. There are four primary interpretive positions:

**First** — the war is symbolic only, representing God's ultimate victory over Satan and Israel's enemies in general. No literal event is intended.

**Second** — the war occurs in our present age, before the Tribulation begins.

**Third** — the war occurs during the Tribulation period and is connected to the events of Armageddon.

**Fourth** — the war occurs at the end of the millennial Kingdom, after the thousand years of Christ's reign are completed.

The text itself provides the evidence to evaluate each view. As we move through Chapter 38, the specific details — the condition of Israel, the nature of the weapons, the absence of Arab nations, the geographic and topographic descriptions, and the Lord's stated purpose — all converge on a single conclusion. That conclusion will be drawn from the text, not imposed upon it.

## The Actors — Gog of Magog

> **Ezekiel 38:1-6 (NASB1995)**
> *"And the word of the LORD came to me saying, 'Son of man, set your face toward Gog of the land of Magog, the prince of Rosh, Meshech and Tubal, and prophesy against him and say, "Thus says the Lord GOD, 'Behold, I am against you, O Gog, prince of Rosh, Meshech and Tubal. I will turn you about and put hooks into your jaws, and I will bring you out, and all your army, horses and horsemen, all of them splendidly attired, a great company with buckler and shield, all of them wielding swords; Persia, Ethiopia and Put with them, all of them with shield and helmet; Gomer with all its troops; Beth-togarmah from the remote parts of the north with all its troops — many peoples with you.'"*

The place names here trace back to the Table of Nations in Genesis 10. Japheth's descendants settled in the region broadly north and northeast of Israel — present-day Turkey, and the nations extending from it into Eastern Europe and Central Asia: Syria, Georgia, Armenia, and further regions that today include Russia. The word *Gog* is a title, not a personal name — like Pharaoh or Caesar. It identifies a ruler, not an individual. The oracle is directed at whoever holds that title and rules that region in the day these events unfold.

His coalition is drawn from every direction surrounding Israel: Persia from the east — present-day Iran. Ethiopia and Put from the south and southwest — the region of northeast Africa. Gomer and Beth-togarmah from the north. Notably, no Arab nation appears in the list. The nations that most immediately surround Israel today and have historically been her most hostile neighbors are entirely absent. That absence is not accidental, and it becomes a significant timing clue when placed against the Kingdom prophecies of Chapters 34–37, which promise that Arab nations will never again threaten Israel.

## The Most Important Actor

Before examining the nations, the most important detail in these opening verses must not be missed. It is the Lord Himself.

> **Ezekiel 38:4 (NASB1995)**
> *"I will turn you about and put hooks into your jaws, and I will bring you out, and all your army."*

The Lord does not merely *permit* this invasion. He *instigates* it. He puts hooks in the jaws of Gog and pulls this coalition toward Israel at a time of His own choosing. This is the sovereign decree of God at work — the same decree that governs every event in heaven and earth, down to the sparrow and the lot cast into the lap.

> **Proverbs 21:1 (NASB1995)**
> *"The king's heart is like channels of water in the hand of the LORD; He turns it wherever He wishes."*

> **Proverbs 16:33 (NASB1995)**
> *"The lot is cast into the lap, but its every decision is from the LORD."*

This raises an immediate theological question that the text itself anticipates: if the Lord instigates the invasion, does that make Him the author of Gog's sin? The answer is no — and Scripture is clear on why.

> **James 1:13-15 (NASB1995)**
> *"Let no one say when he is tempted, 'I am being tempted by God'; for God cannot be tempted by evil, and He Himself does not tempt anyone. But each one is tempted when he is carried away and enticed by his own lust. Then when lust has conceived, it gives birth to sin; and when sin is accomplished, it brings forth death."*

God ordains the timing and the outcome. The sin belongs to Gog. The mechanism the Lord uses is revealed in Revelation 20 — He releases Satan from his thousand-year imprisonment at a time of His sovereign choosing, and Satan goes out to deceive the nations, stirring up the desire in Gog's heart that was already there. The Lord appoints the moment. Satan does the deceiving. Gog acts on his own greed. Each bears exactly what Scripture assigns to him — no more, no less.

> **Revelation 20:7-8 (NASB1995)**
> *"When the thousand years are completed, Satan will be released from his prison, and will come out to deceive the nations which are in the four corners of the earth, Gog and Magog, to gather them together for the war; the number of them is like the sand of the seashore."*

This is the Lord's sovereign governance of evil — not authorship of sin, but providential use of it for His own redemptive and revelatory purposes. He did the same at Calvary.

> **Acts 2:23 (NASB1995)**
> *"This Man, delivered over by the predetermined plan and foreknowledge of God, you nailed to a cross by the hands of godless men."*

Godless men nailed Him. God predetermined it. Both statements are true. The same logic applies here.

## The Condition of Israel — A Timing Marker

> **Ezekiel 38:8 (NASB1995)**
> *"After many days you will be summoned; in the latter years you will come into the land that is restored from the sword, whose inhabitants have been gathered from many nations to the mountains of Israel which had been a continual waste; but its people were brought out from the nations, and they are living securely, all of them."*

Four precise conditions describe Israel at the time of this invasion:

**All of Israel is in the land.** Not some. Not a portion. *All of them.* The regathering is complete. This alone eliminates any identification with present-day Israel, where a significant portion of the world's Jewish population remains outside the land.

**The land has gone from desolation to abundance.** The mountains of Israel, which had been a continual waste, are now inhabited and prosperous — consistent with everything Chapters 35 and 36 promised about the land of the Kingdom.

**Israel is dwelling securely, without walls, without bars or gates.** This is the most decisive detail for timing. Present-day Israel is one of the most defensively fortified nations on earth. Its walls are not symbolic — they run for miles. Its defense forces are among the most sophisticated in the world, and the nation maintains a constant state of military readiness. The Israel of Ezekiel 38 has no walls, no military, and no expectation of attack whatsoever.

**Israel is the center of the world.** The text says they *"live at the center of the world"* (v.12). Geographically and Biblically, Israel has always been at the center of God's purposes — but by any contemporary measure of political power, economic influence, or cultural weight, Israel is not the world's center today. In the Kingdom, that changes entirely.

> **Zechariah 8:20-23 (NASB1995)**
> *"Thus says the LORD of hosts, 'It will yet be that peoples will come, even the inhabitants of many cities. The inhabitants of one will go to another, saying, "Let us go at once to entreat the favor of the LORD, and to seek the LORD of hosts; I will also go." So many peoples and mighty nations will come to seek the LORD of hosts in Jerusalem and to entreat the favor of the LORD.' Thus says the LORD of hosts, 'In those days ten men from all the nations will grasp the garment of a Jew, saying, "Let us go with you, for we have heard that God is with you."'"*

That is Israel at the center of the world — not today, but in the Kingdom. And that is the Israel Gog invades.

## Why Gog Comes — Opportunity and Greed

> **Ezekiel 38:10-13 (NASB1995)**
> *"Thus says the Lord GOD, 'It will come about on that day, that thoughts will come into your mind and you will devise an evil plan, and you will say, "I will go up against the land of unwalled villages. I will go against those who are at rest, that live securely, all of them living without walls and having no bars or gates, to capture spoil and to seize plunder, to turn your hand against the waste places which are now inhabited, and against the people who are gathered from the nations, who have acquired cattle and goods, who live at the center of the world."'"*

Gog sees two things: vulnerability and wealth. The land is unguarded — no walls, no gates, no bars, no military apparatus of any kind. In the Kingdom, the Lord rules so perfectly that war has become obsolete. Isaiah said as much:

> **Isaiah 2:2-4 (NASB1995)**
> *"Now it will come about that in the last days the mountain of the house of the LORD will be established as the chief of the mountains, and will be raised above the hills; and all the nations will stream to it. And many peoples will come and say, 'Come, let us go up to the mountain of the LORD, to the house of the God of Jacob; that He may teach us concerning His ways and that we may walk in His paths.' For the law will go forth from Zion and the word of the LORD from Jerusalem. And He will judge between the nations, and will render decisions for many peoples; and they will hammer their swords into plowshares and their spears into pruning hooks. Nation will not lift up sword against nation, and never again will they learn war."*

In a world where warfare has been entirely forgotten, where no nation trains armies or manufactures weapons, Israel has no defenses because she needs none. The Lord is her defender. But Gog, deceived by Satan and driven by his own greed, sees only the opportunity. The wealth of Israel in the Kingdom will be second to none — and Gog wants it.

Notice that other nations observe the invasion with apparent bewilderment rather than joining it:

> **Ezekiel 38:13 (NASB1995)**
> *"Sheba and Dedan and the merchants of Tarshish with all its villages will say to you, 'Have you come to capture spoil? Have you assembled your company to seize plunder?'"*

Sheba and Dedan represent Arabia to the east. Tarshish represents maritime trade from the west. They ask the question with what sounds like surprise — not participation. In a Kingdom age where no one has contemplated war in a thousand years, the spectacle of an uncountable army assembling with wooden weapons would be genuinely unprecedented and remarkable to any observer.

## The Weapons — Wood, Not Metal

The text is specific about the armaments: horses, bucklers, shields, swords, bows and arrows — all of wood, as Chapter 39 confirms when the burning of the weapons is described as providing Israel with seven years of fuel.

The Hebrew word for *horse* means horse. There is no interpretive mechanism in the text to render it symbolic. And this detail, which strikes modern readers as strange, makes complete sense in the Kingdom context. Isaiah describes transportation in that age:

> **Isaiah 66:20 (NASB1995)**
> *"Then they shall bring all your brethren from all the nations as a grain offering to the LORD, on horses, in chariots, in litters, on mules and on camels, to My holy mountain Jerusalem."*

In the Kingdom, animals are the mode of transportation and labor. The art of war — metallurgy for weapons, engineering for siege equipment, tactics for modern combat — has been lost over a thousand years of peace. When Gog assembles his army, he assembles it with the only tools anyone knows how to make: wood, leather, and horses.

This detail by itself eliminates the view that this war occurs in our present age. No nation with access to tanks, aircraft, missiles, and armored vehicles would invade with horses and wooden spears. And if they did, Israel's defense forces would end it before the army crossed the border.

## The Lord's Purpose — Sanctified Before the Nations

> **Ezekiel 38:14-16 (NASB1995)**
> *"Therefore prophesy, son of man, and say to Gog, 'Thus says the Lord GOD, "On that day when My people Israel are living securely, will you not know it? You will come from your place out of the remote parts of the north, you and many peoples with you, all of them riding on horses, a great assembly and a mighty army; and you will come up against My people Israel like a cloud to cover the land. It shall come about in the last days that I will bring you against My land, so that the nations may know Me when I am sanctified through you before their eyes, O Gog."'"*

The Lord states His purpose plainly. He is not caught off guard by Gog's invasion. He ordained it. And the reason He ordained it is so that the nations may know Him when He is sanctified through the defeat of Gog before their eyes.

Two purposes emerge from the chapter, and both are rooted in the covenant structure of Chapters 33–48.

The **first purpose** is to demonstrate His faithfulness to the peace covenant made with Israel in Chapter 37. He promised that Israel's peace would not be taken. Gog comes to take it. The Lord intervenes to defend it. And in that defense, the covenant is proven — not declared, but *proven*, before watching nations.

The **second purpose** is to make Himself known to the nations of the world who, even in the Kingdom, do not all acknowledge Christ as Lord. In the millennial Kingdom, natural men and women continue to be born, live, and die. They have original sin. They require Christ's rule precisely because sin remains. And faith — confidence in what is not seen — is still required of them. Jesus is not visible in the streets of the Kingdom. He reigns from the temple. Those born into that world will grow up in an age when everything functions differently than what we know, but to them it is simply normal. The word of God is still the evidence they must trust.

> **Psalm 110:1-2 (NASB1995)**
> *"The LORD says to my Lord: 'Sit at My right hand until I make Your enemies a footstool for Your feet.' The LORD will stretch forth Your strong scepter from Zion, saying, 'Rule in the midst of Your enemies!'"*

Christ rules in the midst of enemies during the Kingdom — until the last enemy is placed under His feet. The Gog war is that final act. After it, there are no more enemies. The purpose of the Kingdom is fulfilled.

## The Lord's Response — Fury, Earthquake, Fire

> **Ezekiel 38:18-23 (NASB1995)**
> *"It will come about on that day, when Gog comes against the land of Israel," declares the Lord GOD, "that My fury will mount up in My anger. In My zeal and in My blazing wrath I declare that on that day there will surely be a great earthquake in the land of Israel. The fish of the sea, the birds of the heavens, the beasts of the field, all the creeping things that creep on the earth, and all the men who are on the face of the earth will shake at My presence; the mountains also will be thrown down, the steep pathways will collapse and every wall will fall to the ground. I will call for a sword against him on all My mountains," declares the Lord GOD. "Every man's sword will be against his brother. With pestilence and with blood I will enter into judgment with him; and I will rain on him and on his troops, and on the many peoples who are with him, a torrential rain, with hailstones, fire and brimstone. I will magnify Myself, sanctify Myself, and make Myself known in the sight of many nations; and they will know that I am the LORD."*

Israel does nothing. The people remain securely in their unwalled villages. The Lord fights this battle entirely on their behalf — just as He fought for Israel at the Red Sea, and just as He confounded the enemies of Israel throughout her history when she trusted Him rather than her own military strength.

The Lord's response is catastrophic and comprehensive. A planet-wide earthquake. The armies turned against one another in confusion. Torrential rain, hailstones, fire and brimstone. The entire invading force is destroyed without Israel lifting a sword.

The effect is the open, public sanctification of the Lord before all the nations of the earth:

> **Ezekiel 38:23 (NASB1995)**
> *"I will magnify Myself, sanctify Myself, and make Myself known in the sight of many nations; and they will know that I am the LORD."*

Earlier prophets had spoken of this day. The Lord reminds Gog of that:

> **Ezekiel 38:17 (NASB1995)**
> *"Thus says the Lord GOD, 'Are you the one of whom I spoke in former days through My servants the prophets of Israel, who prophesied in those days for many years that I would bring you against them?'"*

> **Psalm 110:5-6 (NASB1995)**
> *"The Lord is at Your right hand; He will shatter kings in the day of His wrath. He will judge among the nations, He will fill them with corpses; He will shatter the chief men over a broad country."*

This is not a new plan improvised in response to an unforeseen uprising. This is the culmination of what the Lord declared from the beginning — the final placement of every enemy under the feet of the Son.

## Looking Ahead to Chapter 39

Chapter 38 brings the invasion to an abrupt end. Chapter 39 carries the narrative into its consequences — the burning of the wooden weapons for seven years, the seven months of burial, and the Lord's declaration of His glory among the Gentile nations. But the purpose that drives Chapter 39 is already stated here at the close of Chapter 38: *the nations will know that I am the LORD.*

Chapter 39 is the first half of the final section of the book — the Lord dwelling among His people. It addresses how the Lord makes Himself known to the Gentile nations through the aftermath of the war. Chapters 40–48 then address how the Lord dwells among Israel in His restored temple forever.

What the covenant of peace promised in Chapter 37 is proven in Chapters 38 and 39. What is proven in Chapters 38 and 39 becomes the permanent reality of Chapters 40–48. The structure is deliberate. Each chapter is load-bearing.

> **1 Corinthians 15:24-26 (NASB1995)**
> *"Then comes the end, when He hands over the kingdom to the God and Father, when He has abolished all rule and all authority and power. For He must reign until He has put all His enemies under His feet. The last enemy that will be abolished is death."*

The Gog war of Ezekiel 38 is the Lord's final act of putting enemies under the feet of Christ before death itself is abolished and the Kingdom is handed to the Father. Everything in these chapters is moving toward that consummation. The Lord has been declaring it from ancient times:

> **Isaiah 46:9-10 (NASB1995)**
> *"Remember the former things long past, for I am God, and there is no other; I am God, and there is no one like Me, declaring the end from the beginning, and from ancient times things which have not been done, saying, 'My purpose will be established, and I will accomplish all My good pleasure.'"*

Chapter 39 will show how the Lord ensures that no nation remains unaware of what He has done — and why He did it.
